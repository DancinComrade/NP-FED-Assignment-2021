Vulnerabilities

No software is perfect. They are all developed by humans, and humans make mistakes, which introduces bugs and glitches. As such, attackers take advantage of these loopholes to discover and exploit novel vulnerabilities, which in turn, leads them to your system or data.

Safe Browsing Practices

Did you know that 98% of all cyber attacks rely on social engineering? (Purplesec, 2021) Well, now you know! Malware don't just magically appear on your machine, more often than not, some form of user intervention is made and those pesky bugs got in. That's why, as users, we have to be responsible for our own actions and know what we are actually clicking on.

Layers of Security

Naturally, as humans, we all make mistakes. Regardless of how tech-savvy you are, sometimes, you might just simply overlook something, and the bugs got in. So, why not let your own device help you instead?

Vulnerabilities

Keep software up to date.

Software such as Microsoft Office and Adobe Suite or even your Windows operating system release updates regularly, from quality-of-life improvements to major security enhancements. Though frequent updates may seem annoying, but they are absolutely crucial in order to secure your device and data.

Remove legacy or unused programs.

An outdated software is ticking time bomb, waiting to go off, as with no more security patches being released, vulnerabilities will never be patched, coupled with poor and reliability, your system is left with a bunch of loopholes waiting to be exploited.

Enable click-to-play plugins.

Besides unpatched loopholes, malware can also make their way into your device through malicious advertisements. As such, even without any user intervention, a malware can take advantage of this and load itself automatically onto your device, should you allow plugin animations like the good ol' Flash Player to run by itself.

Safe Browsing Practices

Watch for malicious links and websites.

Likewise, if you stumble upon malicious sites, not only will it contain malicious popups and advertisements, it may also contain malware and exploit kits, which is a collection of exploits, such as Nuclear, and Angler/Axpergle. (levinec, Dansimp, get-itips, RAJU2529, & Dani, 2021) So do ensure the site has https, with a valid certificate, and even check online reviews of the site before proceeding.

User Access Control.

In the event when a malware is launched, most malware will run with the same privileges as the user account that initiated it. As such, it is recommended to use a non-adminstrator when browsing the internet. With limited permissions, malware are unable to make devastating changes on the device, and delete your files.

Hash and Scan.

Before running a file that is downloaded from a credible online source, it is best to hash the downloaded file and comapare its hash to the one from the online source. This ensures that the downloaded file is exactly the same as the one from the source and it is not altered, which may occur duing a Man-in-the-middle Attack (MIM). After which, scan the file with a decent antivirus software to ensure that the file is indeed safe to run.

Firewall

A firewall is defined as a software or hardware that helps to protect your network by monitoring your network traffic for potential malicious activities. As such, a firewall is important as it helps to secure your network and data by preventing malicious inbound traffic from approaching your devices and network. (Alison , 2021)
